# Rubric Mapping

This document maps competition scoring criteria to evidence we should prepare.

## Preliminary Round

### 1. Necessity And Problem Definition - 20 Points

What judges look for:

- clear problem in the drug development process,
- proper use of domain knowledge from pharmaceutical, chemical, medical, or clinical fields.

What we should prepare:

- a narrow problem statement,
- current workflow explanation,
- why the problem affects time, cost, quality, or safety,
- Medical IT/HIS relevance,
- 3-5 credible references.

Risk:

- If the problem is too broad, it may look like a general chatbot.

### 2. Originality And Creativity Of Agent Design - 20 Points

What judges look for:

- autonomous planning and tool use beyond a simple chatbot,
- differentiated architecture such as multi-agent collaboration or feedback loops.

What we should prepare:

- agent role diagram,
- step-by-step workflow,
- tool-use plan,
- self-check or critic loop,
- audit trail design.

Risk:

- Overcomplicated architecture without a clear workflow can look unrealistic.

### 3. Technical Feasibility - 20 Points

What judges look for:

- realistic implementation plan,
- concrete use of open-source models or external libraries such as RDKit.

What we should prepare:

- minimal viable prototype scope,
- public API/database list,
- library list,
- deployment plan,
- implementation timeline.

Risk:

- Claiming large-scale model training or full clinical validation is not realistic for this project.

### 4. Evaluation Set And Metrics - 10 Points

What judges look for:

- appropriate evaluation set and metrics for agent performance.

What we should prepare:

- 3-5 sample scenarios,
- checklist-based scoring,
- citation correctness score,
- missing-item detection score,
- human review rubric.

Risk:

- If evaluation is only "looks good to me", it will be weak.

### 5. Business And Social Value - 20 Points

What judges look for:

- expected time/cost reduction,
- practical value in pharmaceutical, clinical, or research settings.

What we should prepare:

- target users,
- before/after workflow comparison,
- expected efficiency gains,
- hospital or clinical research adoption path,
- limitations.

Risk:

- Value claims must be conservative and tied to actual workflow pain points.

### 6. Research Ethics And Completeness - 10 Points

What judges look for:

- transparent AI use,
- research ethics and completeness.

What we should prepare:

- AI-use disclosure,
- no diagnosis/treatment claim boundary,
- data privacy boundary,
- citation and audit logging plan,
- limitation section.

Risk:

- Medical projects can become risky if they imply clinical decision-making without validation.

## Current Strategic Interpretation

For a Medical IT portfolio, the proposal should emphasize:

- traceability,
- workflow integration,
- data readiness,
- regulatory awareness,
- conservative safety boundaries,
- practical implementation.

The strongest current candidate is still:

> Clinical Trial Protocol Review Agent

because it can connect drug development with hospital information systems, clinical research workflows, and regulatory review without requiring us to pretend we can fully validate new molecules.
